Famous Connections

Royalty · Legends · Presidents · Icons

Four hundred years of documented ancestry reveals some remarkable relatives — from Mayflower passengers and Native American royalty to rock & roll legends and Founding Fathers. There's an important distinction: some are direct ancestors Barrett is literally descended from, and others are famous cousins who share a common ancestor.

📖 WHAT DOES "ONCE REMOVED" ACTUALLY MEAN?

This trips up nearly everyone — but once you get it, it clicks forever. "Removed" has nothing to do with how distant the relationship is. It's purely about generational difference.

When two people are in the same generation — like two people who both share a great-grandparent — they are simply cousins: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and so on. No "removed." The cousin number tells you how far back you have to go to find the shared ancestor.

"Once removed" means there is exactly one generation of difference between the two people. So Barrett's 1st cousin's child is Barrett's 1st cousin once removed — same cousin relationship, but one rung lower on the generational ladder. Go two rungs down and it's "twice removed." That's it.

Think of it this way:

Relationship What it means
4th cousin Same generation — you both share a 4× great-grandparent. Neither is older or younger in the family tree.
4th cousin, once removed One of you is one generation older or younger than the other. The cousin number still describes the type of relationship; removed just accounts for the generational offset.
4th cousin, 8× removed Eight full generations apart — like Barrett and George Washington. Washington was born in 1732; they share the same ancestral line, just in wildly different eras of history.

The bottom line: "Removed" is a calendar correction, not a measure of closeness. A 3rd cousin once removed is not farther away than a plain 3rd cousin — they're just from a different generation of the same branch. When you see "8× removed," that's simply history catching up — centuries of generations separating the same shared bloodline.

A NOTE ON THE RESEARCH

All connections on this page are sourced from Barrett's documented Ancestry family tree and cross-referenced against public genealogical databases. Direct ancestors are individuals who appear in the unbroken parent-child chain from Barrett back through history — their DNA literally contributed to his. Famous cousins share a common ancestor but branched into separate family lines generations ago. Neither type of connection is more remarkable than the other — but they mean very different things genealogically.
